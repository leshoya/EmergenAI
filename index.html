The future of emergency response and EMS navigation

with AI prediction

Using real-time analysis from drone and aerial inputs, our platform can predict and optimize emergency response routes, ensuring that first responders reach their destinations as quickly and safely as possible.

Real-Time Route Optimization

Emergency vehicle GPS mapping uses live traffic data to suggest the fastest and safest routes, helping responders avoid congestion and road closures.

Location Tracking

Dispatch centers can monitor the exact location of emergency vehicles, improving coordination and response times during critical incidents.

Incident Integration

GPS mapping systems can integrate with incident data, automatically guiding vehicles to precise locations such as accident scenes or patient addresses.

Historical Data Analysis

Collected GPS data enables agencies to analyze response patterns, identify bottlenecks, and improve future emergency planning and resource allocation.

The Initiative

A new Raleigh Fire master plan reveals the Raleigh Fire Department consistently misses national targets for emergency response times, with only 54% of first units arriving within the recommended timeframe and only 48% meeting effective response force goals.
